Straight answers

Investor FAQ

Is this gambling?

No. Get Lucky is a purely skill-based competition. The outcome of a hole-in-one challenge is determined entirely by golfing skill under standard competition rules — no randomness, no wagering, no odds-based payouts. Entries are competition entries, outside traditional gambling regulation, and every prize is fully insured and pre-funded with clear verification protocols.

What if someone actually wins R1,000,000?

We celebrate loudly — and the insurer pays. Every prize is underwritten (Santam/Indwe), pre-funded and verified by dual cameras plus an AI shot tracer. A big winner is not a risk event for the business; it's the best marketing day of the year.

How do the course partnerships work?

Exclusive, long-term deals: courses earn 10% of every paid entry with zero upfront cost, on 2-year auto-renewing agreements with a restraint of trade. Get Lucky covers installation, insurance, technology and operations. Clubs have moved faster than expected — GMs need member engagement and new non-intrusive revenue, and we hand them both.

Why only one hole per course?

We install on the signature par-3 to concentrate attention, participation and brand impact — and expand by agreement where demand justifies it. The Metropolitan and Boschenmeer already operate two live challenge holes each.

Who else does this? What's the moat?

An always-on, permanently installed, AI-verified hole-in-one platform is a world first. The only meaningful local competitor was acquired and rebranded into Get Lucky at launch. Globally, hole-in-one products are one-off event promotions, not platforms. The moat: speed of rollout, exclusive locked-in course contracts, owned verification tech and the WhatsApp-native journey — now compounded by Ernie's name on the category.

The deck shows two entry models — how do they fit together?

The premium installed model (dual cameras, physical entry board) anchors the brand at top-tier courses and earns its 78% margin. The phone-based global model runs entirely on the golfer's phone and scales to any par-3 on earth with no installation cost and no revenue share. Installs create legitimacy; phone entries create exponential reach.

What stops golfers from entering?

Confidence, not interest. Money-back-if-you-hit-the-green promo days reframed the decision and doubled entries (~25% of entrants hit the green). The WhatsApp platform removes the remaining friction: one-tap re-entry, stored payment details, no forms at the tee.

What exactly am I buying in this round?

Equity in Get Lucky Golf Club (Pty) Ltd: 10% of the company for R4m at a R40m post-money valuation, alongside founders holding 90% and Ernie Els's founding 5% stake. Full terms, the cap table and the driver-based valuation model are in the dataroom.
